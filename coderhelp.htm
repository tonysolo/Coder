Coder Help

Coder is a program that allows healthcare providers to code their patients' healthcare visits and store data into cloud storage. The idea is for all healthcare providers to share data that is interesting for everyone and essential for public health planning without disclosing patient identifable information or their specific practice identifiable data.

Presently only claims sent to medical schemes are coded and the data is used for checking claims. Coder offers a way to collect and share healthcare data on a much larger scale, combining coding with geographical and other essential data without compomising patient confidentialty.

Templates

Visits contain only general information so Coder gives you the facility to save Visits as Templates for coding future Visits. Templates are just named Visits without dates that you can list separately. You can select a Templates to generate a new visit. Its a very quick and accurate way to generate data.

Places

Coder uses low resolution GPS coordinateCoder uses low resolution GPS coordinates to record where patients come from and where they are treated.

We can provide the names and coordinates of major towns to import using File>Import_places. These go into the Places list for viewing and editing, You should add your practice region and the places of your referals. Use decimal  decimal degree coordinates. Coder rounds them to 0.1 degree accuracy.

How it works

Coder compiles and lists Visits files and provides the means to synchronise them with a Internet server usisng a webservice. The server files all the Visits from all practices together by appending them to 'day_region' files.  are uses to store data (10 km map resolution)connects to webservice to store data on a webserver.  . When you synchonize your practice data, all unsaved records are sent to the webservice, the appropriate day_region  file opened or created and the data appended to it, one line per visit. The web service return  the file length  and Coder uses this to compute the file position of each visit which allows you to edit your data on the server..

After allowing a month for late insertions and editing, the day files are sealed as a  'read_only' and together with other day files they form a permanent data warehouse.
